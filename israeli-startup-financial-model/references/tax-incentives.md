# Israeli Startup Tax Incentives

## R&D Tax Credits (Hok HaIdud / חוק העידוד)

### Encouragement of Capital Investment Law
- Provides reduced corporate tax rates for qualifying enterprises
- Application through IIA for technology enterprise classification

### Preferred Enterprise (Mifal Muadaf / מפעל מועדף)
- **Corporate tax:** 7.5% in Zone A (periphery), 16% elsewhere
- **Dividend withholding:** 20%
- **Requirement:** Export revenue exceeds 25% of total revenue
- **Application:** Through Israel Tax Authority with IIA confirmation

### Preferred Technology Enterprise (Mifal Technologi Muadaf / מפעל טכנולוגי מועדף)
- **Corporate tax:** 7.5% in Zone A, 12% elsewhere
- **Dividend withholding:** 4% (foreign company parent), 20% (Israeli parent)
- **Requirements:**
  - IP-based income
  - R&D expenditure at least 7% of revenue (3-year average)
  - One of: R&D employees > 20% of workforce, or VC investment > 8M NIS, or revenue growth > 25%

### Special Preferred Technology Enterprise
- **Corporate tax:** 6%
- **Dividend withholding:** 4%
- **Requirements:** Group revenue exceeds 10 billion NIS

## Angels Law (Section 20 / סעיף 20 - חוק האנג'לים)

### Eligibility Requirements - Company
- Israeli company (registered in Israel)
- R&D-focused (majority of expenses on R&D)
- Under 5 years from incorporation at time of investment
- Annual revenue under 50 million NIS
- Not publicly traded

### Eligibility Requirements - Investor
- Individual investor (not a company)
- Will hold less than 49% of the company after investment
- Not an employee or officeholder of the company (some exceptions)

### Tax Benefit
- Investment amount deductible against any income (not just capital gains)
- Deduction spread over 3 years (1/3 each year)
- Maximum qualifying investment: check current year limits (was 5M NIS)
- Holding period: must hold shares for minimum period (typically 3 years)

### Process
1. Company obtains approval from IIA as "Target Company" (Chevra Matara)
2. Investor makes investment
3. Investor claims deduction on personal tax return
4. Must retain documentation for Tax Authority review

## Employee Stock Options - Section 102

### Trustee Track (Capital Gains Route)
- Options/shares held by trustee for 24 months
- Gain taxed at 25% (capital gains rate)
- Employer gets no deduction
- Most common in Israeli startups

### Trustee Track (Income Route)
- Options/shares held by trustee for 12 months
- Gain taxed as ordinary income (up to 50%)
- Employer gets matching deduction

### Non-Trustee Track
- No holding period
- Taxed as ordinary income at exercise
- Rarely used in practice

### Key Considerations
- 102 plan must be filed with Tax Authority 30 days before first grant
- Trustee (nominee) must be approved Israeli trustee company
- Exercise price, vesting schedule, and other terms defined in plan
- International employees may have different tax treatment

## Practical Tips

### For Financial Models
- Tax benefits reduce effective tax rate significantly
- Preferred Enterprise status can drop corporate tax from 23% to 7.5-16%
- Angels Law can accelerate fundraising (investor incentive)
- Section 102 affects ESOP pool sizing (factor in tax treatment)

### Common Pitfalls
- Preferred Enterprise requires consistent export revenue
- Angels Law approval must be obtained before investment
- Section 102 trustee period is strict (early release = income tax)
- IP transfer out of Israel can void Preferred Enterprise benefits
