# IIA (Innovation Authority) Grant Programs

## Overview

The Israel Innovation Authority (Rashut HaHadshnut / רשות החדשנות) provides grant funding for R&D activities in Israel. Grants create royalty obligations and IP restrictions.

## Grant Programs

### R&D Fund (Keren Mechkar / קרן מחקר)
- **Rate:** 20-50% of approved budget
- **Reporting:** Semi-annual financial and technical reports
- **Duration:** 1-2 year programs, renewable
- **Eligibility:** Israeli company with R&D activity in Israel
- **Budget categories:** Salaries, subcontractors (up to 50%), materials, equipment depreciation, overhead (20-25% of direct costs)

### Early-Stage Incubator (Chashamanit / חממה)
- **Rate:** Up to 85% of approved budget
- **Duration:** Up to 2 years
- **Eligibility:** Must be in an IIA-approved incubator
- **Special:** IP jointly owned with incubator operator during program

### Binational Programs
- **BIRD Foundation:** US-Israel, up to 50%, max $1M per project
- **BSF (Binational Science Foundation):** Research grants
- **CIIRDF:** Canada-Israel, similar structure to BIRD

### Multinational Collaboration
- **Rate:** 30-50%
- **Partners:** EU (Horizon Europe), India, China, Korea, Singapore
- **Requirement:** Collaboration with approved foreign entity

## Royalty Obligations (Tamlugim / תמלוגים)

When a company receives IIA grants and generates revenue:
- **Rate:** 3-5% of product revenue (as defined in the grant approval)
- **Cap:** Total royalties repaid = grant amount received (linked to USD)
- **Timeline:** Royalties due from first revenue until fully repaid
- **Reporting:** Annual royalty reports to IIA

## IP Restrictions (Haghbalot Kinyan Ruchani)

- IP developed with grant funding must remain in Israel
- Transfer of IP outside Israel requires IIA committee approval
- Approval may require payment of 3-6x the grant amount
- Manufacturing transfer may also require approval
- These restrictions apply even after royalties are fully repaid

## Application Process

1. **Submit application** through IIA online portal (ezor ishi)
2. **Budget preparation:** Detailed budget by category, monthly breakdown
3. **Technical plan:** R&D milestones and deliverables
4. **Committee review:** Expert committee evaluates (takes 2-4 months)
5. **Approval letter:** Specifies approved budget, grant rate, conditions
6. **Execution:** Begin approved R&D activities
7. **Reporting:** Submit reports per schedule in approval letter

## Budget Categories and Limits

| Category | Typical Limit | Notes |
|----------|--------------|-------|
| Salaries (employee) | No specific cap | Based on approved headcount |
| Subcontractors | Up to 50% of budget | Must be pre-approved |
| Materials and supplies | As needed | Must be R&D related |
| Equipment depreciation | Depreciation only | Not full purchase price |
| Travel | Limited | Must be R&D related |
| General overhead | 20-25% of direct costs | Flat rate on direct costs |

## Common Mistakes

- Including marketing expenses in R&D budget (not eligible)
- Claiming salary above approved ceiling per employee
- Late report submission (can trigger grant clawback)
- Failing to report changes in R&D plan (must get pre-approval)
- Not tracking co-funding requirement separately
