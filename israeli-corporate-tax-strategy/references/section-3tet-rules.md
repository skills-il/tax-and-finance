# Section 3(tet) and 3(yod) -- Deemed Interest Rules

## Legal Basis

Sections 3(tet) and 3(yod) of the Israeli Income Tax Ordinance establish tax liability on loans provided at below-market interest rates between related parties.

## Section 3(tet) -- Non-CPI-Linked Loans

**2026 prescribed rate:** 6.53%

If a loan or credit is provided without interest or at interest below 6.53%, the difference is deemed taxable income to the borrower. For controlling shareholders, deemed interest is classified as employment income and reported on Form 126.

## Section 3(yod) -- CPI-Linked Loans

**2026 prescribed rate:** 4.9%

For loans between related parties that are CPI-linked, minimum real interest of 4.9% applies.

## Deemed Dividend Reclassification Risk

The Tax Authority may reclassify a shareholder loan as a deemed dividend when:

| Risk Factor | Impact |
|-------------|--------|
| No written loan agreement | High risk |
| No repayment schedule | High risk |
| Loan used for personal purposes | Medium-high risk |
| Company has distributable retained earnings | Medium risk |
| Loan balance grows year over year | High risk |

**Consequences:** Entire loan taxed as dividend at 30% + potential surtax + interest and penalties.

## Documentation Requirements

1. Written loan agreement signed by both parties
2. Interest rate at least 6.53% (or documented reason for lower)
3. Repayment schedule with specific dates
4. Board resolution approving the loan
5. Proper bookkeeping entries
6. Evidence of actual repayments
